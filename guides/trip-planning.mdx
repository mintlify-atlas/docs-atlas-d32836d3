## Create your trip

TripLoom builds a personalized planning workspace in seconds.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter destination">
    From your dashboard, enter your destination in the search field. TripLoom supports cities, regions, and airport codes.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose date mode">
    Select how you want to handle dates:
    - **Exact** - Specific start and end dates
    - **Weekend** - Quick weekend getaway
    - **Flexible** - Browse options before committing
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select travelers">
    Choose **Solo** for individual trips or **Group** for travel with others. You can adjust the exact traveler count later.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create trip">
    Click **Create Trip** to generate your workspace. TripLoom automatically calculates total days and sets up your planning flow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your trip appears immediately in your dashboard. All changes save automatically as you work.
</Note>

## Set trip basics

Once created, configure your core trip details from the Overview page.

### Edit trip details

1. Navigate to **Overview** in your trip sidebar
2. Click **Edit Trip** to open the trip editor
3. Update any field:
   - Destination name
   - Start date (YYYY-MM-DD format)
   - End date (must be on or after start date)
   - Number of travelers (minimum 1)

<Warning>
  Changing dates recalculates your total trip days. This affects itinerary planning and budget pacing.
</Warning>

### Track progress

The Overview page shows your completion status across seven planning stages:

- **Trip Basics** - Destination, dates, travelers
- **Flights** - Outbound and return flight selection
- **Hotels** - Accommodation booking
- **Itinerary** - Day-by-day activity planning
- **Transit** - Local transportation
- **Finance** - Budget and expense tracking
- **Ready** - All essential items complete

Your progress bar updates automatically as you complete each section.

## Follow the guided workflow

TripLoom's "Next Best Action" card guides you through planning in optimal order.

### Understanding recommendations

The system analyzes what you've completed and suggests your next step:

- **Title** - Clear action to take next
- **Description** - Why this step matters now
- **Recommendations** - Specific suggestions (up to 3 bullets)
- **CTA button** - Direct link to the relevant page

<Tip>
  The recommended path prioritizes critical booking tasks first (flights, hotels), then moves to detailed planning (itinerary, finance).
</Tip>

## Check what's missing

The "What's Missing" card on Overview lists incomplete essential items:

- Flight selection (outbound or return)
- Hotel booking
- Itinerary days not yet planned
- Budget not set
- Major expenses not tracked

Once all essential items are complete, you'll see a confirmation message with a checkmark icon.

## Monitor recent activity

The "What Changed" section shows your most recent actions across all trip pages. Use this to:

- Verify recent edits saved correctly
- See what collaborators changed
- Track progress over multiple sessions

## Quick summary cards

Overview displays four summary cards for at-a-glance status:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Flights">
    Shows flight selection status and route summary (e.g., "YYZ → LHR, LHR → YYZ").
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Hotel">
    Displays hotel selection status and area (e.g., "Shoreditch, London").
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Itinerary">
    Shows days planned vs. total days (e.g., "5/7 days planned").
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Budget">
    Displays total budget and per-person estimate (e.g., "$3,500 total • $1,750/person").
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Share and collaborate

From the Overview page, use the top action buttons:

- **Share** - Generate a shareable link for view-only access
- **Invite** - Send edit invitations to co-travelers

Group features unlock automatically when you set travelers > 1 or enable group mode in Finance settings.

## Complete your trip

Work through each section in the sidebar:

1. **Flights** - Search and save flight options
2. **Hotels** - Find and track accommodation
3. **Itinerary** - Build day-by-day plans
4. **Transit** - Research local transportation
5. **Finance** - Set budget and track expenses
6. **Group** - Manage members and approvals (if applicable)
7. **Docs** - Store confirmations and documents

When your progress reaches 100%, all essential planning is complete and you're ready to travel.
